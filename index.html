Rad'a Castle

Description:Rad'a Castle is one of the historical Yemeni castles, located in the center of the city of Rad'a and it is the highest place in it, and it is considered the second most important site in Rad'a after the summit of Mount "Ahrim". Its construction dates back to the era of the Himyarite king Shamir Yuhar'ish, in the year 243 AD before Islam. It was later restored and rebuilt by King Dha'fir Amir bin Abdulwahab Al-Taheri.

Aden Tanks

Description: The Aden tanks or the long tanks in the city of Crater, Sayrah district, specifically in a valley known as Wadi Al-Tawila, extending along a diagonal line from the northwest of the city of Crater - Aden, which is located below the outlets of the high Aden plateau, about 800 feet above sea level.

Aden

Description: Aden is a Yemeni city located on the coast of the Gulf of Aden and the Arabian Sea in the south of the country. It is the economic capital of Yemen and the second most important Yemeni city after Sana'a. Aden has witnessed important historical events and is known as the eye of Yemen.

Tarim

Description: Tarim is a city and the capital of the Tarim District in the Hadhramaut Governorate of the Republic of Yemen. Its history dates back to the 4th century BC, when it was the capital of ancient Hadhramaut and the seat of the Kings of Kindah before the emergence of Islam. It is known for its numerous mosques, with the number reaching around 360, equivalent to the number of days in a year. It is also renowned for its knowledge and religious scholars. The city was chosen as the capital of Islamic culture in 2010 by the Islamic Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization.

Shibam

Description: Shibam is an ancient town and the center of the Shibam district in the Hadhramaut Governorate in eastern Yemen. The walled city, dating back to the 16th century, represents one of the oldest and best-preserved examples of precise urban organization based on the principle of vertical construction.

Socotra

Description: Socotra is a Yemeni archipelago that belongs to the Hadhramaut region, consisting of six islands in the Indian Ocean off the coast of the Horn of Africa near the Gulf of Aden.

Throne of Bilqis

Description: The Throne of Bilqis or the Temple of Bar'an is the most famous archaeological site among the ruins of Yemen, located 1400 meters to the northwest of Ma'rib Bilqis. It is a Sabaean temple and is as important as the Temple of Awwam, locally known as "Al-Amayid.".

Dar Al-Hajar

Description: Dar Al-Hajar is one of the historical buildings in Yemen and is located in Sana'a - Wadi Dhahr. It was built during the 18th century AD by Ali bin Saleh Al-Amari and was named after the granite rock on which this house was built.

Bani Matar Waterfall

Description: Bani Matar Waterfall is a natural resort crowded with visitors throughout the year, reaching its peak during holidays and vacations, especially on festive occasions around the cold waterfall waters.
